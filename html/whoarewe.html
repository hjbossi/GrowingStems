Hannah Bossi is a PhD student at Yale University and an NSF GRFP Fellow. She studies nuclear physics working on the ALICE experiment which is a detector at the world's largest machine; the Large Hadron Collider. There, she studies the results of extremely hot and dense heavy-ion collisions which are designed to recreate the conditions of the early universe. Outside of physics, Hannah is a fan of almost anything outside. Whether it's running, hiking, yoga, rock climbing, or skiing - she is always up for some time outside with friends!


Email: hannah.bossi@yale.edu, Twitter: Follow @HannahBossi, Personal Website

Abby Johnson is a Design Engineer for the Seattle based structural engineering firm Magnusson Klemencic Associates (MKA). She received her Bachelor’s in Physics and Mathematics from Colby College in 2018 and a Master’s in Civil Engineering with a concentration in Structural Engineering from Stanford University in 2020. She is currently working for the office group at her firm and is in the middle of finalizing the Construction Drawings and structural design for a 600 ft high rise that will be added to the Bellevue Washington skyline in the coming year. Her interest in buildings, physics and mathematics was fostered early with the help of an exceptional high school physics teacher. Outside of the world of STEM, Abby loves running, Boston sports, drawing, and spending as much time outside with friends as possible.
